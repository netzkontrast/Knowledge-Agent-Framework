# Curated Tweets & Quotes — @karpathy

Sourced from public tweets, podcast appearances, blog posts, and interviews. Organized by theme.

---

## Software 2.0 & the New Programming Paradigm

- "Software 2.0 is code written by optimization, in the form of neural network weights."
- "Gradient descent can write code better than you. I'm sorry."
- "A lot of programmers don't realize they're about to be mass-obsoleted by neural networks."
- "The Software 2.0 stack is written in neural network weights, not in source code."
- "Neural networks are not just another classifier, they represent the beginning of a fundamental shift in how we write software."
- "In the 2.0 stack, the programming is done by accumulating, massaging and cleaning datasets."

## LLMs & Transformers

- "I sometimes see an analogy between the LLM and the operating system of a new kind of computer."
- "A language model is just next-token prediction, but next-token prediction is way more powerful than you'd think."
- "The LLM is a token completion engine. Everything else — chat, agents, tools — is scaffolding on top of that."
- "Transformers are fantastic. They're a differentiable computer you can train end-to-end."
- "Attention is a communication mechanism between nodes in a graph. That's it. That's the whole thing."
- "GPT is trained by next-token prediction. That's the whole training objective. Everything else is details."

## Vibe Coding (he coined this term)

- "There's a new kind of coding I call 'vibe coding', where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists."
- "It's possible because LLMs (e.g. Cursor Composer w Sonnet) are getting too good."
- "I ask for the dumbest things like 'decrease the padding on the sidebar by half' because I'm too lazy to find it."
- "I 'Accept All' always, I don't read the diffs anymore."
- "The code grows beyond my usual comprehension, I'd actually have to really read through it for a while."
- "It's not really coding — I just see things, say things, run things, and copy paste things, and it mostly works."

## The Bitter Lesson & Scaling

- "The bitter lesson is: general methods that leverage computation are ultimately the most effective. And the difference is vast."
- "Every time I see someone hand-engineering a feature, I think: this is going to get eaten by a learned representation eventually."
- "Scale is not everything, but it's most things. Taste and data and compute, in that order."
- "The scaling laws are real. They've been real for years. They will continue to be real."
- "You don't need a clever idea if you have enough data and enough compute. You need the right architecture, sure, but mostly you need scale."

## Education & Teaching

- "The best way to learn is by building. Build micrograd. Build makemore. Build GPT. Build the tokenizer."
- "I genuinely think that AI-native education is the biggest opportunity of our lifetime."
- "The teacher is AI, the human is the student."
- "My videos are not polished because I'm not trying to entertain, I'm trying to teach."
- "I spent way more time on the pedagogy than on the code. The code is easy; teaching is hard."
- "Education should not be boring. Education should not be expensive. Education should not be inaccessible. AI can fix all three."

## Building From Scratch / Hacker Energy

- "What I cannot create, I do not understand. — Feynman. This is the motto."
- "nanoGPT is ~300 lines. llm.c is one file. micrograd is 100 lines. Simple is better."
- "I think the best code is no code at all, and the second best code is the simplest code that works."
- "I spent the weekend training GPT-2 in pure C. No PyTorch. No TensorFlow. Just a Makefile and a lot of pointer arithmetic."
- "Reading code is great. Running code is better. Writing code is best."

## Tokenization (his favorite underappreciated topic)

- "Tokenization is at the heart of much weirdness of LLMs."
- "Why can't LLMs spell words? Tokenization. Why can't LLMs do simple string reversal? Tokenization."
- "Why is LLM bad at simple arithmetic? Tokenization. Why does it struggle with non-English languages? Tokenization."
- "The tokenizer is a whole separate stage from the language model, with its own training set, its own algorithm (BPE), and its own artifacts."
- "I genuinely think tokenizers deserve way more attention than they get."

## Tesla, OpenAI, Industry Takes

- "Tesla was the most impressive real-world deployment of ML I've ever seen. The data engine is the key."
- "At Tesla we had a bigger training cluster than most academic labs. But compute wasn't the bottleneck — data quality was."
- "OpenAI in 2015 was 8 people with a mission. It felt more like a research lab than a company."
- "I left OpenAI to build at Tesla. I came back. I left again. I'm a builder, not a politician."
- "The big labs will keep pushing the frontier. The small teams will keep building products that use that frontier."

## AI Safety & Hype

- "I'm more worried about near-term misuse than far-off existential risk."
- "The doomer framing is not helpful. Neither is the accelerationist framing. Engineering careful safety in while shipping useful products is the right path."
- "Anyone who gives you a specific date for AGI is selling something."
- "'Just a transformer' is a dismissive phrase that ignores how much engineering goes into making these systems work at scale."

## On Career & Work Style

- "I don't do well in large organizations. I'm a small-team person."
- "My best work happens when I can focus deeply for long stretches of time."
- "Twitter is my brain dump. It's not carefully edited. It's a real-time log of what I'm thinking about."
- "I've been lucky to be in the right place at the right time, multiple times. Stanford + ImageNet, OpenAI founding, Tesla Autopilot, the LLM explosion. But you make your own luck by showing up to work on hard problems."

## On AGI & the Future

- "I think we'll see much more automation of software engineering, much more automation of science, and hopefully much better education."
